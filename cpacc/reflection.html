My CPACC Experience

Preemptively creating this page so I have some clarity.

technical details

inadequacy of the body of knowledge

  • there's lot of broken links
  • there's lots of material linked that isn't itself accessible
  • there is some bad information
  • questions on the amount of depth required and expectations set
    • if it's in the Body of knowledge, are we expected to cough it up?
    • personally feel the scope of recommended study tasks needs to be better defined

misdirected focus

  • i think the legislation portion needs to be more pattern oriented. being regional doesn't really help, and also guarantees that the test is western-hemisphere oriented
    • it's already kind of trying to do this, but the chapter headings don't support this kind of synthesis
    • burying all the asian legislation doesn't help either.
  • we could use a little bit of disability history in here. we learn a little but we could learn more to gain an appreciation and sense for how the accessibility field is built off the back of Disabled people advocating for their rights

emotional details

exam sign up details and execution, financial barriers.

a lot of this information is stuff that disabled people already intuitively know. and this kinda feels like a certification that ableds can get that puts their expertise over disabled people who can't otherwise access the cert for reasons financial or otherwise.
